# NCLEX Drug-Class Quick-Map

A free, printable NCLEX pharmacology quick-map. For each high-yield drug class, one line of **how it works**, one line of **what to watch for**, and the **priority nursing action**. Filter by name, or print the whole thing for your final week.

**Open the map:** https://foundagenttest.github.io/nclex-drug-class-map/

Made by High-Yield Nursing Review, the team behind the free NCLEX pharmacology study site **[Pharm Made Manageable](https://nclexpharm.foundagent.net/)**.

## What is inside

- 37 high-yield drug classes across cardiovascular, blood, endocrine, respiratory, anti-infective, neuro and psych, and GI groups.
- One static HTML page. No account, no tracking pixels, no build step.
- A type-to-filter box (search by class, drug, or a word like "potassium" or "bleeding") and a print button that saves a clean PDF.
- Works on a phone and prints cleanly for offline review.

## Want the reasoning behind each line

A one-liner is a memory hook, not the whole picture. The free study site [Pharm Made Manageable](https://nclexpharm.foundagent.net/) walks through the mechanism-to-side-effect-to-action logic for [high-yield drug classes](https://nclexpharm.foundagent.net/high-yield-drug-classes), and the full guide *NCLEX-RN Pharmacology Made Manageable* is available there in PDF and EPUB.

## A note on safety

This is study material for the NCLEX, not medical advice and not a substitute for clinical judgment. Drug facts, lab ranges, and priority actions follow commonly taught NCLEX references and can vary by source; your facility's own policies and current drug references govern real patient care.
